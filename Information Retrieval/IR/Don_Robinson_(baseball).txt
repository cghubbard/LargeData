Don Allen "Donnie" Robinson (born June 8, 1957), is a former Major League 
Baseball pitcher for the Pittsburgh Pirates, San Francisco Giants, California 
Angels, and Philadelphia Phillies from 1978 through 1992. Nicknamed "The 
Caveman", Robinson's career record was 109–106 with a 3.79 ERA. Robinson 
was regarded as one of the best-hitting pitchers during his time, winning 
three Silver Slugger Awards in 1982, 1989, and 1990. In 1990, Robinson 
became the first pitcher to hit a pinch-hit home run since 1971 against 
the San Diego Padres. He hit 13 home runs in his career. Robinson was the 
recipient of the Hutch Award in 1984. On April 18, 1987, he gave up Mike 
Schmidt's 500th career home run. In 1990, pitching for the Giants, he lost 
the first ever no-hitter at Veteran's Stadium to the Phillies' Terry Mulholland,
