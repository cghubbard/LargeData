The Columbus Buckeyes were a Negro league baseball team that played for 
a single season, 1921, in the Negro National League. Hall-of-Fame shortstop 
John Henry Lloyd was hired as playing manager of the Negro league team 
and Sol White, a manager, player and journalist in African-American baseball 
history, served as coach and general adviser to the team. A young Clint 
Thomas, who later became a successful outfielder, was the team's second 
baseman; fastballer Roy Roberts was the workhorse of the pitching staff. 
The team was not very successful, either on the field or at the box office, 
causing Lloyd to constantly adjust the lineup and seek new players. Eventually 
the club finished seventh out of eight teams with a 25-38 record. At season's
