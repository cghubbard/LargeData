The 2004 San Francisco Giants season was the Giants' 122nd year in Major 
League Baseball, their 47th year in San Francisco since their move from 
New York following the 1957 season, and their fifth at SBC Park. The team 
finished in second place in the National League West with a 91-71 record, 
2 games behind the Los Angeles Dodgers. Barry Bonds became the oldest player 
in the history of the National League to win the MVP Award. It would be 
the last winning season San Francisco would have until 2009.[1] Infielders
