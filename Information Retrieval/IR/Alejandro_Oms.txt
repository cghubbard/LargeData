Alejandro Oms (March 13, 1896 – November 5, 1946) was a Cuban center fielder 
in Negro league baseball and Latin American baseball, most notably with 
the Cuban Stars (East). Born in Santa Clara, Las Villas, he died at age 
51 in Havana. Oms played winter ball in the Cuban League from 1922 to 1946. 
He led the league in batting average three times, in 1924/25 (.393), 1928/29 
(.432), and 1929/30 (.380), and won the Cuban League's Most Valuable Player 
Award in 1928/29. He ranks second all-time for career batting average in 
the Cuban League (behind Cristóbal Torriente) with an average of .345.[1]
