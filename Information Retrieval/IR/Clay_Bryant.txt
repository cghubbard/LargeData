Claiborne Henry Bryant (November 26, 1911 – April 9, 1999) was a Major 
League Baseball player from 1935 to 1940 for the Chicago Cubs. Bryant was 
mainly a pitcher, although in 1939 he played one game as an outfielder. 
His career marks were 32 wins, 20 loses and a 3.73 ERA. His most noteworthy 
year as a major league pitcher was in 1938, when he led the major leagues 
in both strikeouts (135) and walks allowed (125). He compiled a 19–11 record 
with a 3.10 ERA that year. He was a longtime manager in the farm system 
of the Brooklyn/Los Angeles Dodgers, and served as a coach for the Dodgers
