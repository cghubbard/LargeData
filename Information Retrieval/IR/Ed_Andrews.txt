George Edward Andrews (April 5, 1859 – August 12, 1934) was a professional 
baseball player. He was a right-handed second baseman and outfielder over 
parts of eight seasons (1884–1891) with the Philadelphia Quakers, Indianapolis 
Hoosiers, Brooklyn Ward's Wonders and Cincinnati Kelly's Killers. He was 
the National League stolen base champion in 1886 with Philadelphia. For 
his career, he compiled a .257 batting average, with 278 RBIs, 602 runs 
scored, and 205 stolen bases. An alumnus of Case Western Reserve University, 
he was born in Painesville, Ohio and later died in West Palm Beach, Florida
