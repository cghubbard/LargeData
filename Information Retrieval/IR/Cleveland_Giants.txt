The Cleveland Giants were a Negro Leagues baseball team. The team played 
only one year, 1933. Their home games were contested at Cleveland's Luna 
Bowl in Luna Park. In 1933, the struggling Columbus Blue Birds of the Negro 
National League dropped out and were replaced by the Cleveland Giants, 
which was practically the same team. Overall the club went 22-28 and did 
not continue after the year. Bingo DeMoss managed the team, which included 
Bill Byrd (5-6), Frog Redus (.325), SS Leroy Morney (.467) and OF Jabbo 
Andrews. Andrews was third in the NNL with 13 homers and third with a .412
