Roberto Clemente Stadium (Spanish: Estadio Roberto Clemente) is a multi-purpose 
stadium in Carolina, Puerto Rico. It is currently used mostly for baseball 
games and is the home of Gigantes de Carolina. The stadium holds 12,500 
people and was built in 2000. It is named after former Puerto Rican baseball 
great and native of Carolina, Roberto Clemente. It hosted the 2007 Caribbean 
Series. From 2008 it is planned to be the home of the Gigantes de Carolina
