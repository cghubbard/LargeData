The Joe Bauman Home Run Award, formerly known as the Round-Tripper-Award, 
is given to the minor league baseball player who hit the most regular season 
home runs that year. The award, first given in 2002, is named after Joe 
Bauman, who set a then-professional record with 72 home runs in 1954, while 
playing for the Roswell Rockets of the Class-C Longhorn League.[1] The 
Joe Bauman Trophy was inaugurated in 2004.[2] It is presented by Musco
