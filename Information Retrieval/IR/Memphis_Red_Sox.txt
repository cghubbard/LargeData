The Memphis Red Sox were a professional Negro league baseball team based 
in Memphis, Tennessee from the 1920s until the end of segregated baseball. 
The Red Sox played in the first Negro National League for most of the League's 
existence, although they also played independently, and in the Negro Southern 
League, before becoming charter members of the new Negro American League 
in 1937. The team did not perform as well in the new league as its roster 
would suggest. For the greater part of its history the team was owned by 
J. B. Martin and B.B. Martin of Memphis, brothers who both maintained dental 
practices and other business enterprises. The brothers built Martin Park
