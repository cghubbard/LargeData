The 1996 Philadelphia Phillies season was a season in Major League Baseball. 
The Phillies finished fifth in the National League East with a record of 
67 wins and 95 losses. They also hosted the 1996 Major League Baseball 
All-Star Game. The 1996 Major League Baseball All-Star Game was the 67th 
playing of the midsummer classic between the all-stars of the American 
League (AL) and National League (NL). The game was held on July 9, 1996 
at Veterans Stadium in Philadelphia. The game resulted in the National 
League defeating the American League 6-0. Joe Carter, the Toronto Blue 
Jays representative to the All-Star Game, received boos from the crowd 
for his home run that ended the 1993 World Series.[11][12][13] Infielders
