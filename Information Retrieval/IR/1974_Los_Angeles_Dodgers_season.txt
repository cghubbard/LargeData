Angeles Dodgers (1) This was the tenth year of a Major League Baseball 
Draft. The Dodgers drafted 23 players in the June draft and six in the 
January draft. The top pick was pitcher Rick Sutcliffe from Van Horn High 
School in Independence, Missouri. Sutcliffe would go on to win the 1979 
Rookie of the Year Award. The Dodgers traded him to the Cleveland Indians 
in 1982 but he would remain in the league through 1994. He accumulated 
a record of 171-139, was a three-time All-Star and won the 1984 National 
League Cy Young Award, while with the Chicago Cubs. They also drafted infielder 
Jim Riggleman in the fourth round. While he never made the Majors as a 
player he had a stint as the Dodgers Major League Bench coach and would 
become a successful Major League Manager with four different clubs. [9]
