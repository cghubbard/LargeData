The Cleveland Blues was a Major League Baseball team based in Cleveland, 
Ohio that operated in the National League from 1879 to 1884. In six seasons 
their best finish was third place in 1880. Hugh Daily threw a no-hitter 
for the Blues on September 13, 1883. Besides Daily, notable Blues players 
included Baseball Hall of Fame member Ned Hanlon. The team was purchased 
by Charles Byrne in 1885 for $10,000 and folded into his Brooklyn Grays
