The Cincinnati Reds' 2004 season included the Reds' fourth-place in the 
National League Central division. The Reds finished with a final record 
of 76-86. That earned them fourth place in their division. They finished 
29 games behind the division winner and eventual National League champion, 
the St. Louis Cardinals. The Reds also finished 16 games behind the second 
place team and National League wild card winner, the Houston Astros. The 
Reds finished 13 games behind the third place team, the Chicago Cubs. They 
finished 3½ games ahead of the fifth place team, the Pittsburgh Pirates,
