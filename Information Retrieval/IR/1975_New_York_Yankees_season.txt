WPIX The 1975 New York Yankees season was the 73rd season for the Yankees 
in New York, and the franchise's 75th season overall. The team finished 
with a record of 83-77, finishing 12 games behind the Boston Red Sox. The 
Yankees played at Shea Stadium due to the ongoing renovation of Yankee 
Stadium, which would re-open in 1976. Bill Virdon opened the season as 
Yankees manager, but he was replaced on August 1 by Billy Martin. This 
would be the first of five stints as Yankees manager for Martin. [1] [2]
