The 1962 St. Louis Cardinals season was the team's 81st season in St. Louis, 
Missouri and its 71st season in the National League. The Cardinals went 
84–78 during the season and finished 6th in the NL, 17½ games behind the 
San Francisco Giants. Also in 1962, the Cardinals became the first NL club 
to wear names on the backs of their uniforms that season.[1] Pitcher Bobby 
Shantz and first baseman Bill White won Gold Gloves this year. Infielders
