Frank Sylvester "Silver" Flint (August 3, 1855 – January 14, 1892) was 
a catcher in Major League Baseball. He began his career at age 19 with 
the St. Louis Red Stockings of the National Association, and he played 
one year for the Indianapolis Blues before joining the Chicago White Stockings 
in 1879, where he would remain for the rest of his playing career, eleven 
seasons almost exclusively as a catcher. In his first season with the White 
Stockings, Flint and Cap Anson split duties piloting the team, which finished 
5–12 under Flint and 41–21 under Anson. That was Flint's only opportunity 
as field manager. He was also known as a drinker, and in 1892, he died
