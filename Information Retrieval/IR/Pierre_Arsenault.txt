Pierre Jean Arsenault (born October 12, 1963 in Roberval, Quebec) is a 
Canadian professional baseball scout for the Miami Marlins of Major League 
Baseball and a former longtime bullpen coach and coordinator.[1] He was 
listed as a coach for 16 consecutive seasons for the Montreal Expos (1992–2001) 
and the Florida Marlins (2002–2007).[2] As of 2013, he was serving as one 
of the Marlins' professional scouts, based in Pierrefonds, Quebec.[3] As 
a Marlins coach, he was the first Quebecer to win the World Series in 2003.
