The Pittsfield Electrics were an American minor league baseball club located 
in Pittsfield, Massachusetts. They joined the Eastern Association, a now 
defunct minor professional baseball league, in 1913, and finished in 7th 
place that first year with a record of 62 wins and 73 losses. Pittsfield's 
W.I. Smith led the EA with 175 strikeouts.[1] After the 1914 season, the 
team finished in 6th place with a record of 60 wins and 63 losses, the 
Eastern Association folded. Robert Troy led the EA with 212 strikeouts.[2]
