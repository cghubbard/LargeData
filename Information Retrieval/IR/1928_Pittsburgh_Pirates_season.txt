The 1928 Pittsburgh Pirates season was the 47th season in franchise history. 
The team scored the most runs in the National League. However, they also 
allowed the third most and slipped down to fourth place in the standings. 
During the season, Burleigh Grimes became the last pitcher to win at least 
25 games in one season for the Pirates in the 20th century.[3] [1] [2]
