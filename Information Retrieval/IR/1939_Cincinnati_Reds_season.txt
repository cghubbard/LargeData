The 1939 Cincinnati Reds season was a season in American baseball. The 
team finished first in the National League, winning the pennant by 4½ games 
over the St. Louis Cardinals with a record of 97–57. The team went on to 
the 1939 World Series, which it lost in four straight games to the New 
York Yankees. During the season, Paul Derringer and Bucky Walters became 
the last pitchers to win at least 25 games in one season for the Reds in
