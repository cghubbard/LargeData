as the Washington Statesmen, was a professional baseball team in the mid 
to late 1880s. They existed for a period of four years as a member of the 
National League (NL) from 1886 to 1889. During their four-year tenure they 
had six different managers and compiled a record of 163-337, for a .326 
winning percentage. The franchise played their home games at Swampoodle 
Grounds. Their most notable player was catcher Connie Mack, who went on
