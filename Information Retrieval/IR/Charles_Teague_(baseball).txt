Charles Clyde Teague (born 1924) was an American professional baseball 
player. A second baseman, he played in minor league baseball. As a college 
baseball player for Wake Forest University, he was named an All-American 
in three seasons. In 2010, he was inducted into the National College Baseball 
Hall of Fame. Teague attended Eugene High School. He was named captain 
of his school's baseball team in a vote amongst the players. Teague attended 
Wake Forest University from 1947 through 1950, playing college baseball 
for the Wake Forest Demon Deacons baseball team. He was the first Demon 
Deacon to be named an All-American in baseball, receiving the honor three 
times.[1][2] The Chicago Cubs signed Teague in 1950 and assigned him to
