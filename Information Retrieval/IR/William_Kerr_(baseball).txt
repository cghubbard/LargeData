William Kerr was the co-owner of the Pittsburgh Pirates baseball team of 
the National League from 1891 through 1899 with Phil Auten. They purchased 
the team from William A. Nimick in 1891. They sold the Pirates to Barney 
Dreyfuss in 1900. Kerr was known throughout the organization for his short 
temper. He changed managers frequently during his tenure with the Pirates.
