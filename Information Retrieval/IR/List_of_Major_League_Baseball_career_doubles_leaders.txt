In baseball, a double is a hit in which the batter advances to second base 
in one play, with neither the benefit of a fielding error nor another runner 
being put out on a fielder's choice.[1] A batter may also be credited with 
a ground-rule double when a fair ball, after touching the ground, bounds 
into the stands or becomes lodged in a fence or scoreboard.[2] Hall of 
Fame center fielder Tris Speaker holds the Major League Baseball career 
doubles record with 792.[3] Pete Rose is second with 746, the National 
League record.[4] Speaker, Rose, Stan Musial (725), and Ty Cobb (724) are
