2nd The Greek national baseball team is the baseball team that represents 
Greece in international play. Infielders Outfielders Coaches The first 
time that Greece put together a national team that got any recognition 
in the baseball world was during the 2004 Athens Olympic Games. Greece, 
as the host country, was faced with the dilemma that the actual baseball 
talent pool in the country was very small, and what players were available 
were amateur. However, as a result of lenient citizenship laws, various 
minor leaguers from the United States were able to play for the Greeks. 
Among the better known players were Baltimore Orioles prospect Nick Markakis, 
former Major Leaguer Clay Bellinger and various other players of Greek 
descent. The team's American coach, Dusty Rhodes[1] led them to a 7th-place 
finish in the tournament, with a single win against Italy. Olympic Games
