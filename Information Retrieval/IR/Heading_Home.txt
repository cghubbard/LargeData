Heading Home (also called Headin' Home) is a 1920 American silent sports 
film directed by Lawrence C. Windom.[1] It attempts to create a mythology 
surrounding the life of baseball player Babe Ruth. The screenplay was written 
by Arthur "Bugs" Baer from a story by Earle Browne. Besides Ruth, it stars 
Ruth Taylor, William Sheer and Margaret Seddon. Ruth stars in the film, 
playing himself, but the details of his life are completely fictionalized.
