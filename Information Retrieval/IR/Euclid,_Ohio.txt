Euclid is a city in Cuyahoga County, Ohio, United States. It is an inner 
ring suburb of Cleveland. As of the 2010 census, the city had a total population 
of 48,920.[7] In 2009, Euclid celebrated its bicentennial. The city was 
named after Euclid, the Greek mathematician.[8] The city was settled in 
1796 and became a township in 1809. Euclid then became a village in 1903 
and a city in 1930, during the Great Depression. Euclid is located at 41°35′44″N
