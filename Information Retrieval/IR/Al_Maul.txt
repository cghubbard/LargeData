Albert Joseph "Smiling Al" Maul (October 9, 1865—May 3, 1958) was a professional 
baseball player. He was a pitcher over parts of fifteen seasons (1884–1901) 
with the Philadelphia Keystones, Philadelphia Quakers/Phillies, Pittsburgh 
Alleghenys, Pittsburgh Burghers, Washington Senators, Baltimore Orioles, 
Brooklyn Superbas and New York Giants. He led the National League in earned 
run average in 1895 while playing for Washington. For his career, he compiled 
an 84–80 record in 188 appearances, with a 4.43 ERA and 346 strikeouts.
