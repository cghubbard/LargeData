Danny Bradford Cox (born September 21, 1959) is a former Major League Baseball 
pitcher. He pitched for the St. Louis Cardinals from 1983 to 1988, the 
Philadelphia Phillies from 1991 to 1992, the Pittsburgh Pirates in 1992, 
and then the Toronto Blue Jays from 1993 to 1995, when he retired. Over 
his eleven-year career, Cox won 74, lost 75, recorded a 3.64 ERA, 21 complete 
games, 5 shutouts and 8 saves. He won Game 3 of the 1985 National League 
Championship Series with the Cardinals trailing 2 games to none. Cox pitched 
well in the 1985 World Series, but earned two no-decisions. He pitched 
a shutout in Game 7 of the 1987 National League Championship Series, and 
was the winning pitcher in Game 5 of the 1987 World Series. He managed 
the Gateway Grizzlies, a Frontier League team based in Sauget, Illinois
