Derrel Osborn Thomas (born January 14, 1951) is a former professional baseball 
player who played in Major League Baseball primarily as a second baseman, 
center fielder, and shortstop from 1971 to 1985. He held the distinction 
of being one of a few players to have played every position (except pitcher) 
at least once in his career. Following his playing career, Thomas was the 
first manager of the Boise Hawks in 1987,[1][2] then an independent team 
in the Class A-Short Season Northwest League. As of 2009, he is a member 
of the Los Angeles Dodgers organization serving as a representative of
