Carson Springer Fulmer (born December 13, 1993) is an American professional 
baseball pitcher for the Chicago White Sox of Major League Baseball (MLB). 
He played college baseball for the Vanderbilt Commodores. He was drafted 
by the White Sox in the first round of the 2015 MLB Draft. He was called 
up by the White Sox on July 15, 2016 as a bullpen reliever. Fulmer attended 
All Saints' Academy in Winter Haven, Florida and was drafted by the Boston 
Red Sox in the 15th round of the 2012 Major League Baseball Draft.[1][2] 
He did not sign and attended Vanderbilt University. As a freshman in 2013, 
Fulmer, appeared in 26 games as a relief pitcher, going 3–0 with a 2.39 
earned run average (ERA), four saves and 51 strikeouts in 52 2⁄3 innings. 
Fulmer started his sophomore season in 2014 as a relief pitcher, but was 
moved to the starting rotation in April.[3] After becoming a starter, he 
had a 28 innings scoreless streak.[4] In June, he helped the Commodores 
win the 2014 College World Series against the Virginia Cavaliers after 
allowing only one earned run in 5 2⁄3 innings of the third game.[5] He 
finished the season 7–1 in 26 games (10 starts) with a 1.98 ERA, 10 saves 
and 95 strikeouts in 91 innings, earning the SEC Pitcher of the Year Award.[6] 
After the season, he played for the United States collegiate national team 
during the summer.[7] Fulmer won the National Pitcher of the Year Award.[8] 
He was a finalist for the 2015 Golden Spikes Award, presented annually 
to the nation's top college player.[9] The Chicago White Sox selected Fulmer 
in the first round, with the eighth overall selection, of the 2015 MLB 
Draft.[10] Fulmer signed with the White Sox, receiving a $3,470,600 signing 
bonus.[11] Fulmer was called to the Major Leagues on July 15, 2016.[12]
