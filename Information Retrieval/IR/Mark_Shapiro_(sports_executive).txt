Mark Shapiro (/ˈmɑːrk ʃəˈpaɪroʊ/; born 1967) is an American professional 
baseball executive, currently working as the president and CEO of the Toronto 
Blue Jays of Major League Baseball (MLB). He previously worked with the 
Cleveland Indians from 1991 to 2015, beginning in player development and 
ending as team president. Shapiro worked in the Cleveland Indians organization 
since 1991 when he was recommended by former Tribe GM Hank Peters. He had 
worked his way up from player development director to assistant general 
manager, and in 2001 became general manager.[1] Shapiro was named Executive 
of the Year by the Sporting News in 2005 and 2007,[2] following 90+ wins 
seasons by the Indians, including an American League Central Division Championship 
in 2007.[3] At the end of the 2010 season, he became the Cleveland Indians 
team president, with Chris Antonetti succeeding Shapiro as general manager.[4][5] 
On August 31, 2015, The Toronto Blue Jays announced that Shapiro would 
become their new president and chief executive officer (CEO) at the end 
of the 2015 season, succeeding Paul Beeston.[6][7] Shapiro did not formally 
begin working in the new role until October 31, 2015, the same day that 
Beeston retired.[8][9] In a press conference on November 2, Shapiro named 
Tony LaCava the interim general manager, to replace Alex Anthopoulos.[10] 
Subsequently, on December 3, 2015, Ross Atkins was named as the team's 
sixth general manager, under Mark Shapiro.[11] Shapiro was born to a Jewish 
family,[12] the son of Baltimore attorney and sports agent, Ronald M. Shapiro, 
and the brother-in-law of former Cleveland Browns coach Eric Mangini. He 
graduated from the Gilman School in 1985 and Princeton University in 1989 
with a degree in history.[13] Shapiro married Norwalk, Ohio native[14] 
Lissa Bockrath in January 2001.[15] They have one son, Caden,[16] and one 
daughter, Sierra.[17] Bockrath-Shapiro is a 1995 graduate of The Cleveland 
Institute of Art, and owns an art gallery called Bockrath Gallery.[18] 
They lived in Bentleyville, Ohio while Shapiro worked for the Indians.[19] 
Shapiro was played by actor Reed Diamond in the 2011 film Moneyball.[20]
