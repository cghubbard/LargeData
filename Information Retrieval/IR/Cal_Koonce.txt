Calvin Lee Koonce (November 18, 1940 – October 28, 1993) was an American 
professional baseball player, a right-handed pitcher in the Major Leagues 
from 1962–71 for the Chicago Cubs, New York Mets and Boston Red Sox. Born 
in Fayetteville, North Carolina, he attended Campbell University. Koonce 
stood 6 feet 1 inch (1.85 m) tall and weighed 185 pounds (84 kg). Koonce 
appeared in 334 Major League games pitched, all but 90 as a relief pitcher. 
He allowed 972 hits and 368 bases on balls in 971 innings pitched, with 
504 strikeouts and 24 saves. He recorded 11 saves and a low 2.42 earned 
run average for the 1968 Mets, and was a member of the Mets' 1969 World 
Series championship team, but he was less effective during the regular 
campaign and did not appear in the postseason. After retiring as an active
