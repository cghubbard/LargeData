The 2000 Major League Baseball season ended with the New York Yankees defeating 
the New York Mets in Game 5 of the World Series,[1] known as the Subway 
Series because both fans and the two teams could take the subway to and 
from every game of the series.[2] A record 5,693 home runs were hit during 
the regular season in 2000.[1] Ten teams hit at least 200 home runs each,[3] 
while for the first time since 1989 and only the fifth since 1949 no pitcher
