The 1998 Tampa Bay Devil Rays season was the first season in franchise 
history. This season, they finished last in the AL East division and finished 
the season with a record of 63-99, 51 games behind the World Champion New 
York Yankees. Their manager was Larry Rothschild. Miguel Cairo was the 
last active player remaining from the Opening Day roster, until retiring
