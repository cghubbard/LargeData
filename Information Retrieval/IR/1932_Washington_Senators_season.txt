The 1932 Washington Senators won 93 games, lost 61, and finished in third 
place in the American League. They were managed by Walter Johnson and played 
home games at Griffith Stadium. The 1932 Washington Senators were the last 
team in the 20th century to have 100 triples in one season.[1] [1] [2]
