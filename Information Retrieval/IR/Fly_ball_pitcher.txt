A fly ball pitcher is a type of baseball pitcher who produces an above-average 
number of fly balls, typically by keeping his fastball high up in the strike 
zone and relying on late movement to cause the batter to be unable to make 
solid contact. This designation is constructed around the ground ball fly 
ball ratio, which measures how frequently a pitcher gets batters out on 
fly balls versus ground balls. The downside of a fly ball pitcher is that, 
in a ballpark where the design tends to favor hitters over pitchers (an 
example being Yankee Stadium), a fly ball pitcher will tend to give up
