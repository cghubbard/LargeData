Ferdinand A. "Gus" Abell (died 1928) was one of the original founders of 
the team that became the Brooklyn Dodgers. A Rhode Island casino owner, 
he put up most of the money to form the team in 1883 and also was the leading 
financing behind the building of Washington Park, the home of the ballclub.[1] 
He stuck primarily to the business side of the franchise, letting his partners 
Charlie Byrne, George Taylor and Joseph Doyle supervise the team's actual 
onfield activities. He was the leading force behind the Dodgers merger
