The 1946 St. Louis Cardinals season was a season in American baseball. 
It was the team's 65th season in St. Louis, Missouri and the 55th season 
in the National League. The Cardinals went 96–58 during the championship 
season and finished tied with the Brooklyn Dodgers for first in the National 
League. St. Louis then won a best-of-three playoff for the pennant, 2 games 
to none. In the World Series, they won in 7 games over the Boston Red Sox. 
They won on Enos Slaughter's "mad dash" that gave them a 4–3 lead in the 
8th inning of game 7. First baseman Stan Musial won the MVP Award this 
year, batting .365, with 16 home runs and 103 RBIs. [1] [2] [3] [4] [5]
