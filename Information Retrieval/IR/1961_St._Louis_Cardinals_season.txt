The 1961 St. Louis Cardinals season was the team's 80th season in St. Louis, 
Missouri and its 70th season in the National League. The Cardinals went 
80–74 during the season and finished fifth in the National League. It was 
the last season before the NL went to a 162-game schedule the following 
season to adjust for the new ten-team league. On July 6, with the Cardinals 
at 33–41, manager Solly Hemus was fired and replaced by Johnny Keane. The 
team went 47–33 under Keane. First baseman Bill White and third baseman
