The New York Mets' 2000 season was the 39th regular season for the Mets. 
They went 94-68 and finished 2nd in the NL East, but earned the NL Wild 
Card. They made it to the World Series where they were defeated by their 
crosstown rival the New York Yankees. They were managed by Bobby Valentine. 
They played home games at Shea Stadium. The Mets played in the first game 
held outside of North America on Opening Day. The Mets played the Chicago 
Cubs in front of over 55,000 at the Tokyodome in Japan. The Cubs won the
