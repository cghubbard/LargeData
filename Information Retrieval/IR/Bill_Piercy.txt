William Benton Piercy (May 2, 1896 – August 28, 1951), born in El Monte, 
California, was a pitcher for the New York Yankees (1917 and 1921), Boston 
Red Sox (1922–24) and Chicago Cubs (1926). Piercy helped the Yankees win 
the 1921 American League pennant. In 6 seasons he had a 27–43 Win-Loss 
record, 116 Games (70 Started), 28 Complete Games, 2 Shutouts, 30 Games 
Finished, 610 ⅔ Innings Pitched, 676 Hits Allowed, 362 Runs Allowed, 289 
Earned Runs Allowed, 16 Home Runs Allowed, 268 Walks Allowed, 165 Strikeouts,
