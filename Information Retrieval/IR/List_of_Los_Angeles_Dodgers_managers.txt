the National League Western Division. The Dodgers began play in 1884 as 
the Brooklyn Atlantics and have been known by several nicknames since (including 
the Grays, Grooms, Bridegrooms, Superbas, and Robins), before adopting 
the Dodgers name for good in 1932.[1] They played in Brooklyn, New York
