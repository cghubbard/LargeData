Cliff Anthony Politte (born February 27, 1974) is a right-handed Major 
League Baseball retired relief pitcher. He was selected in the 54th round 
of the 1995 Major League Baseball Draft by the St. Louis Cardinals as the 
1438th player selected.[1] Politte came up to the parent club in 1998. 
He threw the first pitch at the new Roger Dean Stadium in spring training 
that year. From there, he went to the Philadelphia Phillies and then the 
Toronto Blue Jays, before signing with the Chicago White Sox in 2004. He 
won a World Series ring with the White Sox in 2005. He was designated for 
assignment by the White Sox on July 15, 2006, after giving up a home run 
to Bubba Crosby and was released on July 20. On February 14, 2007, the 
Cleveland Indians signed him to a minor league deal. He pitched only eight 
innings for their Double-A team in 2007. On December 21, 2007, the St.
