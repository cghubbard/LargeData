The 1944 Cincinnati Reds season was a season in American baseball. It consisted 
of the Cincinnati Reds attempting to win the National League, although 
finishing in third place. They had 89 wins and 65 losses. On June 10, Joe 
Nuxhall made his major league debut. At age 15, he is (as of the 2010 season) 
the youngest confirmed player ever to play Major League Baseball. He pitched 
just two-thirds of an inning, giving up 5 runs on 2 hits and 5 walks. He 
would not appear in the majors again until 1952. [1] [2] [3] [4] [5] [6]
