William Pearl Rhines (March 14, 1869 – January 30, 1922) was a professional 
baseball player. He was a pitcher over parts of 9 seasons (1890–1899) with 
the Cincinnati Reds, Louisville Colonels and Pittsburgh Pirates. He led 
the National League in ERA twice (1890 and 1896) while playing for Cincinnati.
