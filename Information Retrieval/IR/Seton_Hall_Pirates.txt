The Seton Hall Pirates are the athletic teams representing Seton Hall University. 
They compete as a member of the National Collegiate Athletic Association 
(NCAA) Division I level (non-football sub-level), primarily competing in 
the Big East Conference for all sports since the 1979-80 season.[3][4][5] 
Men's sports include baseball, basketball, cross country, golf, soccer 
and swimming & diving; women's sports include basketball, cross country, 
golf, soccer, softball, swimming & diving, tennis and volleyball. Seton 
Hall canceled football (which was played in Division III) in 1982. On December 
15, 2012, Seton Hall and the other seven Catholic, non-FBS schools announced 
that they were departing the Big East for a new conference.[6] The school's 
athletic director is Patrick Lyons.[7] The program's mascot is The Pirate[8]
