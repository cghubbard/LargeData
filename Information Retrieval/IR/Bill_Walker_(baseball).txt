William Henry Walker (October 7, 1903 – June 14, 1966) was a professional 
baseball left-handed pitcher over parts of ten seasons (1927–1936) with 
the New York Giants and St. Louis Cardinals. He was the National League 
ERA champion twice (in 1929 and 1931) with New York. For his career, he 
compiled a 97–77 record in 272 appearances with a 3.59 ERA and 626 strikeouts. 
He was born and later died in East St. Louis, Illinois at the age of 62.
