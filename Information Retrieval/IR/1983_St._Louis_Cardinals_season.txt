The St. Louis Cardinals 1983 season was a season in American baseball. 
It was the team's 102nd season in St. Louis, Missouri and the 92nd season 
in the National League. The Cardinals went 79-83 during the season and 
finished 4th in the National League East, eleven games behind the NL Champion 
Philadelphia Phillies. They were the first team in the Divisional play 
era to have a losing season one year after winning the World Series. First 
baseman Keith Hernandez, shortstop Ozzie Smith, and outfielder Willie McGee 
won Gold Gloves this year, although Hernandez was traded to the New York
