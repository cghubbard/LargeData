Alonza Benjamin Bumbry (born April 21, 1947) is a former Major League Baseball 
outfielder who played for the Baltimore Orioles and San Diego Padres from 
1972 through 1985. In 2002, Bumbry was inducted into the Virginia Sports 
Hall of Fame. Bumbry was born in Fredericksburg, Virginia, and graduated 
from Ralph Bunche High School in King George, Virginia.[1] He attended 
Virginia State University.[2] Bumbry was drafted by the Baltimore Orioles 
in the 11th round in 1968.[3] He spent 13 of his 14 major-league seasons 
with the Orioles, and played for them in the 1979 and 1983 World Series. 
Bumbry was voted the American League Rookie of the Year in 1973 with the 
Orioles and was elected to the American League All-Star team in 1980. In 
2002, he was inducted into the Virginia Sports Hall of Fame. He served 
as the outfield/baserunning coach for the York Revolution in 2007. Prior 
to his major league career, Bumbry served in Vietnam and was awarded a 
Bronze Star.[4] His son, center fielder Steve Bumbry, was picked by the
