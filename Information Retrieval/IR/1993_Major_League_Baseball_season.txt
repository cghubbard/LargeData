The 1993 Major League Baseball season was the final season of two-division 
play in each league, before the Central Division was added the following 
season, giving both the NL and AL three divisions each. Sixteen years after 
the American League expanded from 12 to 14 teams, the National League finally 
followed suit, with the Colorado Rockies and the Florida Marlins (now the 
Miami Marlins) joining the NL. It was also the first season since 1976 
that both leagues had the same number of teams. The Toronto Blue Jays capped 
off the season by winning their second consecutive World Series title, 
beating the Philadelphia Phillies in six games. The World Series was clinched
